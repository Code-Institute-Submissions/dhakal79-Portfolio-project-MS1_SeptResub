What is coagulation?

Coagulation is a general treatment method to remove from raw water;

  • Suspended and colloidal particles (turbidity)
  • Dissolved matter (e.g., PO 4 3- , colour, natural organic matter (NOM), F - , heavy metals, etc.)

By adding chemicals (coagulants) and followed by further treatment:

Problem of turbidity in water treatment

The presence of colloidal and small suspended particles causes turbid water!! Some (health) problems with turbidity in (drinking) water are:

  • aesthetic objections (we don’t like to drink turbid water),
  • de-sorption of toxic materials (e.g. heavy metals),
  • less effective disinfection due to shielding and followed by release of hidden pathogenic organisms.

How to measure turbidity?

Method-1

In practice, turbidity can be measured through:
Mesuring the suspended solids (SS) concentration [mg/L water]
This can be achieved in three steps such as;

  • Filtration
  • Drying
  • Weighing

How to measure turbidity?

Method-2

With a Turbidity Meter (“turbidity”) [NTU]
Measuring of scatted light under 900 after radiation of a water sample) (A kind of photometry)
There is an almost linear relationship between SS and turbidity. Measuring turbidity is much easier (cheaper) than determination of SS.

How to remove turbidty from water?

Even after years, the residual turbidity due to colloidal particles might still be too much with regard to the quality standard.
applying coagulation prior to sedimentation is a solution
