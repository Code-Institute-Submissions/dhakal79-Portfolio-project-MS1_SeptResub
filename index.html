Unit Operation in Water Treatment

Aim of the Module

The aim of this short course is to present and exchange state-of-the-art knowledge and experience on the technologies and design practices of conventional water treatment systems.

Learning Objectives

Upon completion, the participant should be able to:

  1. Describe the theoretical principles of the unit processes: coagulation, filtration, sedimentation and dissolved air flotation in conventional surface water treatment
  2. Apply theoretical principles to practical aspects of coagulation, filtration, sedimentation, and dissolved air flotation
  3. Determine design parameters for coagulation, filtration, sedimentation, and dissolved air flotation from laboratory experiments
  4. Design a sedimentation tank
  5. Be able to judge the performance of the unit processes:coagulation, filtration, sedimentation, and dissolved air flotation

Module Introduction


Topics covered in the module

Coagulation

Coagulation is a general treatment method to remove suspended fine particles, colour and other constituents from raw water, afteradding chemicals (coagulants) and followed by further treatment

Sedimentation

Sedimentation is the process of allowing particles in suspension in water to settle out of the suspension under the effect of gravity.

Filtration

Filtration is a process that removes particles from suspension in water.

Dissolved air floatation

It is a water treatment process that clarifies water by the removal of suspended matter using dissolved air under pressure and then releasing the air at atmospheric pressure in the flotation tank.
